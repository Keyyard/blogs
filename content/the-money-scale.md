---
title: "The Money Scale"
---
I think money _defines_ who you are, how _skilled_ you are, and how much you are _worth_. It may sounds not right that there are plenty of people who are not wealthy but are very skilled at their profession. However, in a bigger picture, the skills include not just their career skills, but also their abilities in other aspects of life.

Assume that you are a very _talented_ video editor. Yet you are not wealthy as some other lower-skilled inviduals. You may be an expert in video editing, but you lack the ability to find potential clients, build relationship with your customers, or even manage your own finance. I would say it indicates that you are not skilled, you are just a talented person in the industry but not in life.

I think money is a _scale_ that measures how skilled you are in life. It is a _reflection_ of your skills in every aspect of life. If you are not wealthy, you are not skilled. If you are wealthy, you are skilled. It is as simple as that.
