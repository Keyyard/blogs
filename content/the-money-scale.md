---
title: "The Money Scale"
---
I think money _defines_ who you are, how _skilled_ you are, and how much you are _worth_. It may sounds not right that there are plenty of people who are not wealthy but are very skilled at their profession. But, in a bigger picture, the skills are not only their career skills, but also their skills in every aspect of life in general.

Let's say you are a very _talented_ video editor for instance. But you are not wealthy as some other lower-skilled people. You may be very skilled in video editing, but you may not be skilled in finding potential clients, creating relationship with your customers, or even managing your own finance. I would say that means you are not skilled, you are just a talented person in the industry but not in life.

I think money is a _scale_ that measures how skilled you are in life. It is a _reflection_ of your skills in every aspect of life. If you are not wealthy, you are not skilled. If you are wealthy, you are skilled. It is as simple as that.
